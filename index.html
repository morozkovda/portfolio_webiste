Data Science is a Sport?

As a data scientist, I've witnessed firsthand how our field has evolved into a competitive sport. This transformation was propelled by websites like Kaggle, which host competitions that push us to test our skills against time constraints and tough challenges. I'm particularly drawn to these competitions as they're not just about applying technical skills, but also about the thrill and pressure of performing under tight deadlines.

One of my most memorable experiences was participating in a competition to forecast Walmart's unit sales. Our team ranked in the top 30 out of 4983, an achievement that was rewarding even though we didn't win any money prize. This competition required a complete workflow, including data cleaning, transformation, exploratory data analysis (EDA), feature engineering, model selection, and making accurate predictions. Through this experience, I gained invaluable insights and honed my skills in Time Series Analysis and Applied Machine Learning. The techniques and approaches I used are detailed in my notebook, showcasing my methodology and what one can expect from my analysis. This experience solidified my expertise in areas where I excel, providing a foundation for future projects and challenges in data science.
